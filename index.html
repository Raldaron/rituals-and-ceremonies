Rituals and Ceremonies

Soul Leech Capacitor

In the shadowy corners of our dungeon crawls, there exists a peculiar and dreadful artifact known as the Soul Leech Capacitor. This arcane device, typically forged by dark clerics or necromancers, harbors a sinister purpose intertwined with the essence of existence itself.
The Soul Leech Capacitor acts as a bridge—a grim nexus between the living and the spectral. By tapping into the most cherished soul of a target, the device resurrects this spirit, binding it perpetually to the loved one. However, this resurrection is not a mere act of retrieval from the clutches of death; it's a theft, a siphoning of life force that leaves a scar on the plane between life and death.
Once activated, any mere scratch from the entity connected to this capacitor can rip a soul from its corporeal confines, storing this stolen vitality within its depths. This reservoir of ensnared souls becomes a potent source of mana, readily tapped by the owner of the capacitor for their arcane endeavors.
But the existence of such a creature is neither life nor death. They linger in agony, their form a grotesque parody of their former self—perhaps a zombified, featherless shadow of what they once were, surrounded by ethereal, worm-like wisps instead of wings​​.
The use of a Soul Leech Capacitor is a dark testament to the lengths some will go for power. It embodies a stark reminder of the dungeon's cruel realities, where the line between savior and tormentor blurs, and where power is often gained at the expense of another's eternal torment.
